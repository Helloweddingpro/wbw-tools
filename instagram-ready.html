Instagram doesn't have
to be complicated.

The algorithm changes, the trends change, and it can feel like you need to post every day just to keep up. That leads to burnout — and most wedding suppliers either give up entirely or post randomly with no real strategy behind it.

Here's the truth: for a wedding business working in your local area, you don't need to become a full-time content creator. You don't need to go viral. You don't need to master every new feature Instagram throws at you.

You need a simple, repeatable system that does three things.

🎯
Attract the right couples
Use the right keywords so couples find you when they're searching for suppliers at their venue — putting your work in front of the people most likely to book you.
🤝
Build a referral community
Connect with the suppliers you work alongside — photographers, florists, bands, planners — and turn them into a community of referral partners who send work your way.
💕
Hook them with emotion
When a couple lands on your profile, they should feel something. Content that connects emotionally — that speaks to how they want to feel on their wedding day — is what turns a follower into an enquiry.

Three posts a week. Stories after every wedding. Five minutes of genuine engagement every day. Follow this system consistently and it's enough to grow a following, stay visible, and turn Instagram into a steady source of new enquiries.

Your bio is your
most important post.

Most wedding suppliers treat their Instagram bio like a business card. They put their name, maybe a few emojis, and a link. That's a missed opportunity.

Your bio has two jobs: get found and make someone feel something. The name field is pure SEO — it's what Instagram searches. The bio text is emotion — it's what makes a couple stop and think "that's exactly what I want."

Instagram's search works off your name field — not your username. A couple searching "wedding videographer Waterford" will find suppliers whose name field contains those exact words. Most suppliers don't know this.

Here's a real example
of what works.

This is the bio that attracted consistent enquiries from couples at the right venues, at the right budget.

Wedding Videographer - Waterford | Kilkenny
Capturing Family Bonds, Laughter with Friends, Intimate Moments between you as a couple 💕 Without You Even Knowing We Are There!

📍 Waterford, Ireland · www.mdmvideo.com

Notice what this does: the name field tells Instagram exactly what this person does and where. The bio text doesn't describe a service — it describes a feeling. The CTA is clear and direct. The location is set. The link goes straight to an enquiry form.

Three posts a week.
One simple formula.

Every post you make should do one thing: put your work in front of couples getting married at your best venues. The way you do that is by using the venue name and location as keywords — in the on-screen text of your video and in the first line of your caption.

When a couple searches "wedding photographer Ashford Castle" on Instagram, they find suppliers who use those words consistently. That's the entire strategy.

You don't need to write a long caption. You need the right words in the right places. The venue name and location in the first line of your caption is worth more than a paragraph of generic text.

Get into the habit of capturing content on every wedding day. You don't need to film anything elaborate — a few quick clips or photos of what's going on around you is enough. Create a folder on your phone and label it with the couple's name and venue (e.g. "Sarah & Tom — Ashford Castle"). This makes it easy to find your content when you're ready to post, and you can reuse it across all three posts that week.

Posting is only
half the job.

The Instagram algorithm rewards accounts that are active — not just posting, but engaging. Five minutes a day of genuine connection with other suppliers in your area will do more for your reach than any hashtag strategy.

📱
Post a Story at every wedding
You don't need to edit it. A raw clip from the day — the venue, the setup, a moment — posted as a Story keeps your account active between your three weekly posts. Stories also show couples that you're working regularly. Post it the day of or the day after the wedding.
🤝
Connect with every supplier you work with
After every wedding, follow every supplier you worked alongside on Instagram. Leave a genuine, specific comment on one of their recent posts — not "great work 🔥" but something about what you actually saw them do on the day. This builds real relationships and puts your name in front of their audience.
💬
5 minutes a day — long comments on supplier posts
Every day, find 2–3 posts from other wedding suppliers in your area and leave a long, genuine comment — at least 4–5 words, ideally a sentence or two. This signals to Instagram that you're an active, engaged account. It also puts your name in front of suppliers who may refer you. Do not use generic comments. Be specific about what you see in their post.
📍
Tag the venue location on every post
When you post, always add the venue as a location tag — not just in the caption, but using Instagram's actual location feature. Couples who browse a venue's location feed will find your content. This is free visibility to exactly the right audience.

This system works because it's consistent and targeted — not because it's complicated. Three posts a week using the same venue keywords, stories after every wedding, and five minutes of daily engagement. That's it. Do it for 90 days and you will see the difference in your enquiries.
